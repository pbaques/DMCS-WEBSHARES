What is a webshare?

A webshare is a shared website, a virtual space of asynchronous, collaborative creativity. A cluster of pages (there are four templates) align according to the required functionality. The functional modules are created by linking and threading the four basic page templates. 

WEBSHARE group members (called participants) create and edit the webshare pages, generating a multi-layer collaborative space with a diverse range of applications. I have conceived and designed webshares for editorial projects (one of note: writing for math textbooks), for management purposes (management of restaurant staff at Indiana University, for example) and currently I have set up demos for a course for conference interpreters, for entrepreneurship at the Jacobs School of Music and for the School of Education at IU.

While webshare uses are numerous and diverse, its wiring, or “webchitecture”, (a term I coined) is fairly straightforward. Participants learn and apply it very quickly. A form, a video, an image, and Google Drive elements such as docs, sheets, presentations, or even an entire folder, are inserted in a webshare page to suit collaborative and creative needs. 

Pages host a Comments section where threads of messages can be viewed by all participants in what amounts to a “private Twitter”. Any element of a webshare can be linked to or from any other, and top-down links keep participant-created pages together. A webshare map shows the current storyboard, and a recent-activity page records all edits showing date, time and name of the editor. The entire webshare can be searched using names or keywords.

Webshare functionality is flexible and tailored to the purpose of the group. Participants are responsible for growing the space and adapting design as needed. This level of engagement fosters a unique sense of collective ownership of the content presented in the course.
